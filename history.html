1989 – Chapter Reestablishment

A renewed cohort of architects formally reestablished the Manila Corinthian Chapter (MCC) to uphold professional standards, promote ethical practice, and strengthen public trust in architecture through outreach and education.

1992 – Membership & Practice Support

Expanded membership through peer networks and practice clinics — providing guidance on licensure, contracts, and site supervision.

1995 – First Annual Symposium

Hosted the inaugural Architecture Symposium to explore sustainable urban development, catalyzing multi‑disciplinary dialogue among practitioners, educators, and policy advocates.

1997 – Community Build & Outreach

Launched hands‑on community design service and shelter initiatives, integrating pro‑bono design, stakeholder consultation, and student participation.

2002 – Heritage Conservation Initiative

Began heritage mapping and conservation awareness drives, encouraging adaptive reuse and documentation of built legacies.

2005 – CPD & Technical Masterclasses

Formalized regular CPD‑aligned seminars covering building technology, codes, and professional ethics, raising competency across the chapter.

2008 – Going Online

Launched a web presence and newsletter to share chapter updates, open calls, and research references with members and partners.

2010 – Digital Design Awards

Recognized excellence in digital workflows — from visualization to parametric explorations — highlighting design rigor and clarity in communication.

2014 – International Linkages

Pursued knowledge exchange with local and international collaborators through joint lectures, critiques, and studio partnerships.

2016 – 5‑Time Model Chapter

Achieved recognition as a five‑time Model Chapter — reflecting sustained programs in professional development, community service, and organizational leadership.

2018 – Youth Mentorship Program

Initiated structured mentorship that paired seasoned architects with students and young professionals, strengthening portfolios, ethics, and practice readiness.

2020 – Remote Practice & Community Response

Shifted swiftly to remote seminars and online design reviews while supporting communities through relief design support and information sharing.

2021 – Hybrid Events & Scholarships

Adopted hybrid learning formats and expanded scholarship support — ensuring access to CPD, software literacy, and leadership training.

2023 – Sustainability & Resilience Roadmap

Outlined chapter goals for climate‑adaptive design, circularity, and disaster‑risk reduction — aligning programs with national and global priorities.

2024 – Digitization & Studio Sessions

Streamlined workflows and archives; launched “MCC Studio Sessions” — critique‑driven meetups to sharpen design narratives and technical clarity.

2025 – Virtual Collaboration Platform

Rolled out an online collaboration hub for members to co‑develop resources, mentor remotely, and coordinate events across schedules and geographies.
