The Story of UAP Manila Corinthian Chapter

Every great chapter begins with a spark.

For the UAP Manila Corinthian Chapter (UAPMCC), that spark was lit in the halls of the Technological Institute of the Philippines – Manila. It was 1989, and a group of passionate architecture educators, guided by Ar. Herminio R. Prudente, envisioned a home for teachers and mentors who shaped the next generation of architects.

They first dreamt of naming it the UAP Academe Chapter, but destiny had another title in mind. When the name was deemed too broad, the group embraced a new identity — UAP Manila Corinthian Chapter — a name that would, in time, become synonymous with unity, growth, and excellence.

On September 29, 1989, during the term of UAP National President Ar. Angel R. Lazaro, Jr., the chartering papers were filed, with Ar. Quintin R. Estioko as its charter president. By May 18, 1990, under UAP NP Ar. Richeto C. Alcordo, UAPMCC was officially born. Its first emblem — a proud symbol of its academic roots — was designed by charter member Ar. Annie Corpuz.

From its earliest days, UAPMCC was a small but passionate family. Under the leadership of Ar. Estioko (1990–1992), the seeds of the chapter were planted. Then came Ar. Wilfredo G. Gacutan (1993–1994), guiding the chapter through UAP’s transition from a January–December to a July–June fiscal year, marking the chapter’s first adaptation to change.

Growth followed. Ar. Gabriel Ma. A. B. Cascante (1994–1996) and Ar. Vicente Abe A. Relleve (1996–1998) saw membership expand from just 20 to over 50 members. By the time Ar. Vicente L. Quesea (1998–2000) took the helm, the chapter had opened its doors to PLM graduates, further diversifying its membership. In 1999, UAPMCC also proudly produced its first District B2 Director — Ar. Gabriel Cascante.

The early 2000s marked a period of innovation and tradition-building.
Under Ar. Marlou B. Campaner (2001–2004), the chapter introduced signature programs that still resonate today: PASKOrinthian, Corinthian Kapihan, Corinthian KKK, and the Gawad Gintong Sibol, Yabong, and Ani Awards — all fostering fellowship, recognition, and celebration of excellence. By FY 2003–2004, UAPMCC proudly received its first UAP Model Chapter Award, and two of its pillars, Ar. Gacutan and Ar. Relleve, were elevated to the UAP College of Fellows.

The mid-2000s to early 2010s were years of momentum and recognition.
Ar. Marcial S. Valencia (2004–2006) brought the 2nd and 3rd Model Chapter Awards.

Ar. Anabel Carangan-Valencia (2006–2008) achieved the 4th Model Chapter Award, and later became the 3rd District B2 Director in 2009.

Ar. Pamela Alianza-Constantino (2010–2011) institutionalized the chapter IRR and introduced KEN Estioko Day, honoring the chapter’s roots.

Ar. Ludivico P. Idano (2011–2013) secured the 5th Model Chapter Award, while Ar. Marlou Campaner was elevated to the College of Fellows.

Ar. Omar C. Palero (2013–2015) led the chapter’s Silver Anniversary, a celebration of 25 years of Corinthian pride, while Ar. Patrick Rodriguez became the 4th District Director from UAPMCC.

The next decade became a testament to the chapter’s passion, expansion, and national presence.
Ar. Ma. Sheyne Sanchez-Idano (2015–2016) , Ar. Joy C. Villar (2016–2018) , and Ar. John Paul Manalaysay (2018–2019) saw over 70 plus events and activities flourish, strengthened our network by introducing sisterhood chapter MOAs with Legaspi and Manila Centrum Chapters, and build close ties with Iloilo and Isabela Chapters, More than that they honed the chapter’s professional development and internal policies.

Ar. Reymark Gardiola (2019–2022) - expanded connections and visibility in the national scene, preparing UAPMCC for even greater milestones.

Ar. Michael Adrian C. “Ian” Katipunan (2022–2025) - Ang Presidente “Ian” Katipunan ushered in an era of unparalleled activity, hosting 60+ events per year, strengthening District B2 ties, and earning the UAP Meritorious Award for Outstanding Unity & Camaraderie. During his term, UAPMCC proudly had members take national leadership roles — from NatCon 50 Director to committee chairs and members — marking the chapter as a strong voice in the UAP community.

Through the decades, UAP Manila Corinthian Chapter has grown from a small circle of passionate educators to a vibrant, nationally recognized family of architects, celebrated for its camaraderie, service, and excellence.

Today, as we look back on this journey of over three decades, we see more than just milestones. We see friendships built, traditions born, and a legacy that continues to inspire. And through every president, every project, and every award, one thing remains constant — the Corinthian spirit: united, active, and ever in pursuit of excellence.
