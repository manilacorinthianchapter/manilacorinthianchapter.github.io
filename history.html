Short History of UAP Manila Corinthian Chapter

The UAP Manila Corinthian Chapter started at the Technological Institute of the Philippines Manila Campus and was chartered with the help of Ar. Herminio R. Prudente, former president of UAP Hilaga Chapter and former chairman of Committee on Chapter Organization. (FY January – December 1989).

Its charter members were the teachers and instructors of the Architecture Department of TIP Manila. The name submitted to UAP was UAP Academe Chapter but was rejected since the name was all-encompassing. UAP Manila Corinthian Chapter was chosen as the alternative and accepted.

Chartering papers were filed on 29 September 1989 under UAP NP Ar. Angel R. Lazaro, Jr., with Ar. Quintin R. Estioko as charter president. The chapter was formally inaugurated on 18 May 1990 under UAP NP Ar. Richeto C. Alcordo.

Early fiscal years ran January–December, with Ar. Estioko leading an interim board through December 1989. The chapter logo was designed by Ar. Annie Corpuz, one of MCC’s charter members.
